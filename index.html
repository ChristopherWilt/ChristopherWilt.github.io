About Me

I’ve been holding a controller since I was three years old. In high school, while my friends were at football practice, I was grinding competitive tournaments in CS:GO, Valorant, and Fortnite. That competitive drive naturally evolved into a curiosity about how these games actually functioned. I built my first PC at 15 and wrote my first line of code at 16.

I started simple—HTML and Java—but my time at Full Sail pushed me into the deep end. Moving from high-level Unity and Unreal Engine scripting to Graphics Programming, Linux Systems, and Database Architecture changed my perspective. I realized I didn't just want to make games; I also wanted to engineer the invisible backend systems that make them scalable and responsive.

Off the clock, I’m a dad of two living in Central PA with my fiancé. When I'm not optimizing replication graphs, Im spending time with the family, or I'm usually in the garage. I am currently fixing my BMW E46 325CI's Transmission, but I plan on building a convertible version of the BMW M3 GTR from Need For Speed: Most Wanted. No one has successfully built a convertible version of this car yet, so I plan to be the first once i get the funds to do so. It’s a mechanical nightmare, but just like systems engineering, I love the challenge of making complex moving parts work together.
